Dear Sirs,
With reference to the Engineer's correspondence regarding the alleged failure to achieve Section (1)
Milestone within the contractual timeframe, the Contractor hereby acknowledges receipt of the said notice.
While the Contractor reaffirms its continuous commitment to execute the Works in accordance with the
Contract requirements and the approved construction programme, the Contractor wishes to clarify that the
progress of the Works has been significantly affected by factors beyond the Contractor's control, primarily
the delay in the supply of reinforcement steel by the Employer, in addition to delays and disruptions arising
from changes and conflicts within the IFC Package.
The Contractor had previously issued timely material requisitions for reinforcement steel well in advance
of the required dates in accordance with the approved programme. However, the delay in supply resulted in
a gap between the planned construction sequence and the availability of the required materials, which
Furthermore, the Contractor was compelled to temporarily suspend portions of the Works on site
commencing from 18 March 2026, being the date of issuance of Transmittal Sheet (TRA) No. 017 related
to the revised IFC Package, due to the existence of technical changes and design conflicts requiring
clarification prior to proceeding with execution.
Accordingly, the Contractor submitted Contractor' s Request for Information (RFI) No. 17 dated 11 April
2026 seeking the necessary technical clarifications and instructions. However, the response of the
supervising consultant was only received on 04 May 2026, which resulted in additional delay and
disruption to the progress of the affected activities.
In an effort to mitigate the impact of such delays and avoid disruption to the Works, the Contractor has
taken proactive measures by arranging the supply and delivery of reinforcement steel to the site in stages,
to the extent possible, in line with actual site requirements, with the objective of preventing work stoppages
and minimizing the adverse impact on project progress and continuity.
The Contractor further emphasizes that any assessment of progress, milestone achievement, or
responsibility for delay should take into consideration the cumulative impact of Employer-related delays,
IFC Package revisions, design conflicts, and delayed responses to technical queries affecting critical
activities and the approved construction programme.
