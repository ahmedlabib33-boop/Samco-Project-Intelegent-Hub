Second: Contractual Nature of the Works (Key Point)
Upon reviewing the Contract documents (Contract Agreement, Bill of Quantities, Drawings, and General
Requirements), it has become evident that the backfilling works in this manner and methodology are not
included within the original contractual scope of work, nor are such works measurable or payable under the
existing Bill of Quantities items.
Accordingly, and in accordance with the Contract, these works constitute additional / varied works (a
Variation), and are not an original contractual obligation of the Contractor.
Third: Required Procedure (Per the Contract)
In order for us to proceed with execution without prejudicing our contractual rights (including but not
limited to cost, time, disruption, prolongation and any associated contractual entitlements), kindly issue the
relevant Variation Order for these works, to include the following:
1. Additional Cost, to be valued on the basis of direct cost plus a 25% margin for overheads and profit,
in accordance with Sub-Clause 1.1.4.13 of the Contract.
2. Time Implications (if any), whereby any delay to the critical path arising from the execution of
these works shall be assessed, with a corresponding extension of time granted under Sub-Clause
8.4. For the avoidance of doubt, any delay in issuing the Variation Order shall constitute an
additional ground for extension of time.
3. Measurement and Valuation Procedures in accordance with Sub-Clause 12.1 (based on As-Built
Drawings) and Sub-Clause 12.3 (to the extent applicable and where quantity deviations exceed the
contractual thresholds).
Fourth: Interim Protection of Rights
Pending issuance of the relevant Variation Order, any instruction to proceed with these works shall be
without prejudice to the Contractor's full entitlement to additional cost and/or extension of time under the
Contract, and shall not be construed as acceptance of any obligation to perform varied works at no cost.
